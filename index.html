About Me

My name is Alice. I was born in Saigon, Vietnam and came all the way to Melbourne, Australia to pursue my Master degree in IT. I've just graduated from Monash University few months ago.

In my freetime, I love learning new languages (both programming and human languages) and of course, baking! I must say that I have a real sweet tooth and currently baking is one of my biggest hobbies besides music and badminton. Discovering that I can bake (and you can too!) helps me a lot to face with stressful situations in life. When life gives you lemon, you can bake lemon cake!

Espace means 'space' in French and since I have a background of 16 years learning French, I named my space as Alice's Espace (or L'Espace d'Alice) to store - firstly all the recipes that I love (and sucessfully tried!), and all other things that I can think of later.

Apple Tart

Total time: 1 hour
Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • ♡ 2 medium-sized apples
  • ♡ 1 tablespoon cinamon
  • ♡ 2/4 cup granulated sugar
  • ♡ 8 tablespoons butter
  • ♡ 1/4 cup flour
  • ♡ 1 egg
  • ♡ 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • ♡ 2 sheets thawed puff pastry
  • ♡ 1/4 cup water

Steps

  • 1. Cut the apples into small pieces and set them aside.
  • 2. Melt the butter in a medium heat pan.
  • 3. When the butter is completely melted, add sugar and 1/4 cup water also 1/4 cup.
  • 4. Stir the combination until it well combines, then take the pan out from the heat.
  • 5. Put the apple pieces in a big bowl, add cinamon and mix well until combine.
  • 6. Once the apples are well mixed with cinamon powder, add the butter sugar mixture in the bowl of applies.
  • 7. Mix it well and set it aside.
  • 8. Cut the pastry in 12 equal square pieces.
  • 9. Mix the egg in a small bowl. Use a brush to cover the 4 corners of the pastry pieces, leave the center for the apple filling.
  • 10. Put the apple filling in the middle of 6 pastry pieces, then cover with the other half.
  • 11. Use a folk to seal all the sides of two pastry pieces.
  • 12. Now we have the unbaked apple turnovers, put them in the fridge for 20 mins.
  • 13. In the meantime, preheate the over with 200 degrees Celcius for 10-15 mins.
  • 14. Take the apple tarts out from the fridge, bake in the oven for 25 mins.
  • 15. After 25 mins, your apple tarts are so goo to enjoy!
